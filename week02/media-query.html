Isaiah 4:1-5

In these verses, Isaiah describes difficult times where many men will be lost, causing women to seek security and support in marriage (verse 1). The prophecy then shifts to hope and restoration. Isaiah speaks about the "Branch of the Lord," symbolizing renewal, righteousness, and God's future blessings upon His people (verses 2-3). God promises cleansing, protection, and holiness for those who remain faithful. Verse 5 describes the Lord's presence as a cloud by day and a flaming fire by night, representing divine guidance and protection, similar to how God guided the Israelites in the wilderness.

Mathew 1:23

Gospel of Matthew 1:23 states that a virgin would give birth to a son called Immanuel, meaning "God with us." This verse emphasizes the belief that Jesus Christ's birth fulfilled prophecy and showed that God would dwell among His people.
